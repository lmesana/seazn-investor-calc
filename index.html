Calibration & honesty notes
Per-user dollar values are calibrated against published evidence and scaled by the symptom-management uplift slider, which represents Seazn's reduction in unmanaged symptom burden vs. the status quo. The base scenario assumes a 40% uplift; the slider scales all line items linearly around that baseline. Employer-side savings scale with the % employed slider (base 50%). Public-system, insurer, and women's-pool values are independent of employment status. The four stakeholder pools have some real-world overlap — particularly between employer productivity loss and women's lost income (per ref 3). For investor diligence, the public-health view alone is the cleanest non-overlapping estimate.

Sources & Citations

All figures cited inline. Click superscript numbers above to jump to the source.
  1. UN World Population Prospects (2024) — Global cohort projections. Over 1 billion women globally will be in peri/menopause or postmenopause by 2025, ~500M aged 45–55 by 2030. population.un.org/wpp
  2. Faubion S, et al. "Impact of Menopause Symptoms on Women in the Workplace." Mayo Clinic Proceedings, 98(6):833–845, June 2023. Estimated $1.8B/yr in lost work time and $26.6B/yr when medical expenses included, in the US alone. n=4,440 employed women aged 45–60. mayoclinicproceedings.org
  3. Menopause Foundation of Canada / Deloitte Canada (Oct 2023). "Menopause and Work in Canada." $3.5B annual cost to Canadian economy: $237M in employer productivity loss, $3.3B in lost income to women, 540,000 lost workdays/year. menopausefoundationcanada.ca
  4. CIPD (Oct 2023). "Menopause in the Workplace: Employee Experiences in 2023." Survey of 2,000+ UK women aged 40–60. 67% report negative work impact; 17% considered leaving; 6% have left their job. cipd.org
  5. Fawcett Society (2022). "Menopause and the Workplace." 1 in 10 women who worked during menopause have left a job due to symptoms; 14M working days lost annually in UK; ~£1.8B in lost productivity. fawcettsociety.org.uk
  6. AJMC / Kindra Health. "In the Misdiagnosis of Menopause." 1 in 3 women aged 45–54 have been diagnosed with another condition before menopause was identified. 60% find clinician advice unhelpful. 25% of women 50–65 have never been told they were in peri/menopause despite symptoms. ajmc.com
  7. NAMS / The Menopause Society. 75–85% of women experience menopause-related symptoms; ~25% report severe symptoms affecting quality of life. menopause.org
  8. Grand View Research (2024). Global menopause market projected at $24.4B by 2030, CAGR ~5.4%. Note: this is the addressable product/treatment market, separate from the broader savings opportunity modeled here. grandviewresearch.com
  9. Method notes. Per-stakeholder values reflect distinct economic pools and are presented as additive in the consolidated view (employers capture productivity; women capture out-of-pocket and time; public/insurer pools capture system claims). Real-world overlap exists between employer productivity and women's lost income (per ref 3). For investor diligence, the public-health view is the cleanest non-overlapping estimate.
  10. OECD Employment Outlook 2025 — "Navigating the golden years." Women's labor force participation rates calibrated for women aged ~45–64: OECD avg 66.6%; US ~70%; Canada ~75%; UK ~73%; EU ~63%; Global modeled to ~62% reflecting LMIC mix. Between 2000–2024, employment rose 18.5 percentage points for women aged 55–59 across the OECD. oecd.org/en/publications/oecd-employment-outlook-2025 · LFPR indicator page: oecd.org/en/data/indicators/labour-force-participation-rate
  11. Rock Health — Digital Health Consumer Adoption Survey (2024). Digital health receptivity calibrated against Rock Health's annual consumer survey: 76% of adults 55+ used telemedicine in 2022 (+12 pp YoY); women adopt digital health tools at rates comparable to men; smartwatch ownership reached 37% among women. Base case 60% for "% digitally receptive" reflects active willingness to use a digital health app (not just smartphone ownership), inferred from the Rock Health cohort data. rockhealth.com/insights/consumer-adoption-survey · Women in focus: women as digital health consumers
